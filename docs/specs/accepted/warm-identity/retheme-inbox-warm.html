Orch-monitor · warm re-theme · the identity question

The monitor, re-bound in warm cloth — same spine, new cover.

Below is the Inbox home twice, pixel-for-pixel the same information architecture — sidebar groups, calm header sentence, bare rows, the one bright verb, the reading pane — wearing the living-textbook skin instead of blue graphite. Warm paper and warm charcoal, serif where you read, mono where it's data, and a terracotta accent family where Linear-blue used to be. Live green, needs-you amber and blocked red survive untouched in meaning.

Fig. 1 Warm charcoal — the fleet dashboard runs dark

Calm dark, hue 70

The exact ladder the app already has — chrome, canvas, raised row, card, two border tones, three ink levels — with every stop's lightness kept and the hue swapped blue → warm. Nothing was redrawn: --surface-chrome…elevated just point at warm graphite.

1Serif voice where you read. The wordmark, the calm state-of-things sentence, the ask and the about-block move to the textbook serif; nav, chips and chrome stay quiet sans; keys and durations stay mono.
2Status semantics survive. Blocked keeps its red bar and red verb, decisions keep amber, running rows keep the pulsing live green — the hue swap never touches meaning.
3Terracotta replaces Linear blue. Ticket keys, the active nav item, the brand mark and the one bright verb all draw from the book-cloth accent family — nothing else gets to be loud.
4Same anatomy, recolored. Status glyphs, phase strip, attention pills, presence dots and collapsed-section signal dots are the shipped components, re-inked onto the warm ramp.
Fig. 2 Anthropic paper — the :root theme that already ships, finished

Warm light, on paper

Half of this identity is already in app.css — the #faf7f2 canvas, cream cards, warm borders. What's new here is the other half: the serif reading voice, terracotta in place of the leftover Linear-blue accent, and amber/red/green re-tuned dark enough to hold on paper.

1Identical markup. Both frames render from one template; only the CSS variable scope changes — the proof that this is a token re-theme, not a redesign.
2Accents re-tuned, not re-hued. Amber drops to honey ink on paper, green deepens to moss, red to brick — same roles, AA-legible on cream.
3The chrome stops being a Linear knockoff. Paper surfaces, serif headings and the book-cloth verb read as Catalyst's own voice while every learned behavior stays where it was.
4Cheap to ship. The enforcement contract (board-tokens ⇄ CSS byte-sync, surface paint checks) already polices the ladder — the re-theme is a value swap inside it.
